## Topic Description

In October of 2025, the city of Poza Rica located in Veracruz, México experienced a catastrophic flood which resulted in deaths and property damage. The Mexican government had previously purchased a parametric insurance policy for such geological disasters. However, the index failed to be triggered which resulted in the Mexican government not receiving a payout. Many citizens were left without aid and the Mexican president received intense backlash. In light of this, our financial engineering capstone is to develop models that produce the optimal trigger in order to avoid another payout failure. This area is specifically prone to flooding due to its position near the Gulf of Mexico, river basin geography, low elevation, poor infrastructure, and improper urban planning. Specifically, they have experienced over 40 confirmed floods from 1971-2025. 

Due to limited economic damage data, an impact proxy for floods in the region needs to be developed. The objective for this project is to provide greater context into Poza Rica's exposure and vulnerability through in-depth descriptive analysis. We have decided to utilize spatial analysis to analyze key vulnerability variables: slope, elevation, and proximity to the river. In addition, we have defined exposure to be the number of buildings and building surface area inundated in a floodplain. The hope is to quantify these relationships and be able to answer the following questions: 
- What is the spatial distribution of flood exposure in Poza Rica?
- How are slope, elevation, and proximity to the river associated with floodplain extents?
- What spatial feature is the strongest indicator of whether a building is affected?

This analysis will be incorporated into the impact proxy to add validity within our parametric model design. We are confident that this analysis will surpass the examples in class and push us to develop new data analytical skills.
